Cool Stuff About West Africa

The Land

Check out these amazing places:

  • Awesome beaches along the Gulf of Guinea (super close to sea level)
  • Jungle vibes in the south (gets tons of rain - like 2000mm+ every year)
  • Cool grasslands in the middle (gets medium rain - 500-1500mm)
  • Desert edges up north (not much rain - only 200-500mm)
  • Huge rivers: Niger, Volta, and Senegal (good for boat rides)
  • Cool mountains: Fouta Djallon and Guinea Highlands (great for hiking)

Source: World Bank Climate Change Knowledge Portal, 2023

Weather

Two main seasons:

  • Rainy season (April to October)
    • Most rain in June-September (bring an umbrella)
    • Lots of rain every day (5-10mm)
    • Super humid (80-90%)
  • Dry season (November to March)
    • Cool winds from the Sahara (called Harmattan)
    • Not humid at all (30-40%)
    • Nice temps: 25-35°C

Source: World Meteorological Organization, 2023

Culture

So much cool stuff to see and do:

  • Awesome music and dance
    • Highlife and Afrobeat
    • Traditional drumming
    • Modern music mixing old and new
  • Epic festivals
    • Hogbetsotso Festival
    • Yam Festival (celebrating food)
    • Voodoo Festival (spooky but cool)
  • Cool crafts
    • Kente weaving which is fancy clothing
    • Bronze casting, metal art
    • Beads and pottery

Source: UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage, 2023

Must-See Places

Check out these amazing spots:

  • UNESCO World Heritage Sites (super important!)
    • Cape Coast Castle (Ghana) - old slave trading post
    • Elmina Castle (Ghana) - another important castle
    • Royal Palaces of Abomey (Benin) - ancient kings' homes
  • Old forts and castles
    • Fort St. Sebastian (Ghana) - cool old fort
    • Fort William (Ghana) - more history
    • Fort of São João Baptista (Benin) - Portuguese fort
  • Cool buildings
    • Mud mosques (made of mud but super strong)
    • Traditional Ewe houses
    • Old colonial buildings (European style)

Source: UNESCO World Heritage List, 2023

Our Epic Destinations

Accra, Ghana

Geography

Coastal city with beautiful beaches along the Gulf of Guinea. Features rolling hills and modern architecture mixed with traditional buildings.

Cultural Significance

Capital city and cultural hub of Ghana. Home to the historic Jamestown district and Independence Square, symbolizing Ghana's freedom.

Main Attractions

  • Independence Square and Black Star Gate
  • Jamestown Lighthouse
  • National Museum of Ghana
  • Traditional markets and craft centers

Cape Coast, Ghana

Geography

Historic coastal city with dramatic cliffs overlooking the Atlantic Ocean. Features colonial architecture and fishing communities.

Cultural Significance

UNESCO World Heritage Site. Home to Cape Coast Castle, a major hub of the transatlantic slave trade and symbol of African resilience.

Main Attractions

  • Cape Coast Castle
  • Kakum National Park
  • Traditional fishing villages
  • Local craft markets

Lomé, Togo

Geography

Coastal capital with palm-lined beaches. Features a mix of colonial and modern architecture along the Gulf of Guinea.

Cultural Significance

Major cultural center of Togo, known for its vibrant markets and traditional voodoo practices. Home to the Grand Marché.

Main Attractions

  • Grand Marché (Central Market)
  • National Museum
  • Beach promenade
  • Traditional voodoo ceremonies

Cotonou, Benin

Geography

Major port city on the Gulf of Guinea. Features a lagoon system and modern infrastructure alongside traditional neighborhoods.

Cultural Significance

Economic and cultural hub of Benin. Known for its vibrant art scene and traditional voodoo culture.

Main Attractions

  • Dantokpa Market
  • Fidjrosse Beach
  • Artisan Village
  • Traditional voodoo temples

Travel Safety Information

Health Precautions

Required Vaccinations

  • Yellow Fever (required for entry)
  • Hepatitis A & B
  • Typhoid
  • Meningococcal Meningitis
  • Rabies (recommended)

Malaria Prevention

  • Prescription anti-malarial medication
  • Insect repellent (DEET 30%+)
  • Long-sleeved clothing
  • Mosquito nets for sleeping

General Health Tips

  • Drink only bottled or treated water
  • Avoid street food
  • Wash hands frequently
  • Carry basic first aid kit
  • Travel insurance with medical coverage

Political Stability

Current Status (2024)

  • Ghana: Stable democracy, peaceful elections
  • Togo: Constitutional monarchy, stable government
  • Benin: Democratic republic, peaceful transitions

Travel Advisories

  • Exercise normal precautions in major cities
  • Avoid political demonstrations
  • Stay informed of local news
  • Register with embassy/consulate

Border Security

  • Valid passport with 6+ months validity
  • Required visas for each country
  • Yellow fever vaccination certificate
  • Travel permits for certain areas

Crime Rates

General Safety

  • Petty theft in crowded areas
  • Pickpocketing in markets
  • Scams targeting tourists
  • Vehicle break-ins

Prevention Measures

  • Use hotel safes for valuables
  • Keep copies of important documents
  • Travel in groups or with guide
  • Avoid displaying wealth
  • Use reputable transport services

Emergency Contacts

  • Local police: 191 (Ghana), 117 (Togo), 117 (Benin)
  • Tourist police available in major cities
  • 24/7 tour guide support
  • Hotel security on-site

Research Report

Why We Planned This Trip

We made this 10-day West Africa trip to see cool history stuff, different cultures, and amazing views. We picked places that have important history, awesome cultural things to do, and nice scenery. We wanted the trip to be fun but also safe and comfortable for everyone going.

Places We Picked

We chose these four main places because they have important history, interesting culture, and they're not too hard to get to:

  • Accra, Ghana: It's the big city in charge so it's a good first stop to learn about West African culture and history. You can see old buildings from when Europeans were in charge and new buildings from after they left.
  • Cape Coast, Ghana: This place has castles that UNESCO says are really important, especially Cape Coast Castle where they sent slaves to America. It's also on the ocean so there's nice nature stuff too.
  • Lomé, Togo: You can see weird but cool cultural things here like voodoo ceremonies and traditional markets. The city has lots of different cultures mixed together so it's good for learning about West African traditions.
  • Cotonou, Benin: This place has lots of cultural stuff and traditional art, especially bronze statues and voodoo stuff. There are busy markets and lots of artists so you can really see what life in Benin is like.

Geography Stuff

These places show different types of land in West Africa:

  • Ocean Areas: All these places are next to the Gulf of Guinea, so there are nice beaches and water activities.
  • Weather: It's hot with rainy and dry parts of the year, which changes when you should go and what you can do.
  • Nature: There's all kinds of land from beaches to forests, and each place has different cool nature things to see.

Cultural Important Stuff

Each place has its own special cultural things:

  • History: European countries used to control these places and that changed how buildings look and how people live.
  • Traditional Things: Each city still does old-fashioned customs, from religious ceremonies to art.
  • Modern Culture: Today's West African culture mixes old traditions with new ideas, which makes things interesting.
